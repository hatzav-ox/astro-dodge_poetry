About Us

Explanation for this web application.

The current congressionally directed objective of the NEO Observations Program is to find, track, and characterize at least 90 percent of the predicted number of NEOs that are 140 meters and larger in size–larger than a small football stadium–and to characterize a subset representative of the entire population. Objects of this size and larger pose a risk to Earth of greatest concern due to the level of devastation an impact would cause, and should continue to be the focus of global search efforts. While no known asteroid larger than 140 meters in size has a significant chance to hit Earth for the next 100 years, less than half of the estimated 25,000 NEOs that are 140 meters and larger in size have been found to date.

A web application for tracking space objects is useful for several reasons:

  1. Safety: Knowing the location of space objects can help us avoid collisions with them, which is especially important for spacecraft in orbit. Tracking space debris can help prevent collisions with active satellites and minimize the risk of space junk damaging spacecraft and space stations.
  2. Scientific Research: Tracking space objects can help scientists better understand the dynamics of our solar system and the universe. By monitoring the location, speed, and trajectory of asteroids, comets, and other objects, researchers can learn more about their composition and behavior.
  3. Astronomy: A web application that allows users to track space objects can be useful for amateur and professional astronomers alike. It can help them locate and observe specific celestial objects and plan observing sessions.
  4. Space Operations: Space agencies and companies can use a web application for tracking space objects to monitor the location of their spacecraft and ensure they are on course to reach their intended destinations.

Overall, a web application for tracking space objects is useful for improving safety, advancing scientific research, supporting astronomy, and enhancing space operations.
